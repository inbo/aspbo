American bullfrog

Lithobates catesbeianus


Sander Devisscher

Background

The American bullfrog (Lithobates catesbeianus) is one of the largest frogs in the world and is known to be very competitive and voracious. The species can carry various diseases and poses a threat to native amphibian species as well as biodiversity in general. Currently, there are several populations of the American bullfrog in Flandres, the largest of which can be found in the Grote Nete valley.

The European project LIFE 3n-Stierkikker aims to find a sustainable way to stop the spread of the American bullfrog in Flanders. To this end, the project uses traditional control techniques in combination with the release of sterile frogs.
